Computer networking refers to connected computing devices (such as laptops, desktops, servers, smartphones, and tablets) and an ever-expanding array of IoT devices (such as cameras, door locks, doorbells, refrigerators, audio/visual systems, thermostats, and various sensors) that communicate with one another.

Networking products is one of the most important pieces of IT to manage. Specialized devices such as switches, routers, and access points form the foundation of computer networks. Organizations' internet capabilities as well as software intranet and networking settings must be properly configured to ensure business operations run smoothly.

Modern networks are offerring more than connectivity. Organizations are embarking on digital transformation. Their networks are critical to this transformation and to their success.

Software Development üíª

Happiness and productivity

Creating software is not always easy and never will be. Unhappiness grows exponentially with the complexity of your project, so remember to optimize satisfaction and happiness at every step. There is no universal truth here, but use tools that make you more greater productive, take advantage of libraries and APIs that solve your problems quickly, and write as little code as possible.

No Single paradigm

Do not be constrained by a single paradigm or limited set of ideas. The best practices of these days might be known as anti-patterns tomorrow. End users don't care how you built the product, they care what it does to make their lives better.

Simplicity better than complexity

I like this quote from John Gall: ‚ÄúA complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked.‚Äù

There is a complicated ecosystem of competing in the Operations Support Systems - OSS industry, many of which are built on hype. It is never smooth and easy to navigate, but always ask yourself if this is the best tool for thi job. Just because Tool X has a fancy user interface and is used by Hipster App Inc does now no longer imply you must use it. We generally tend to overdesign everything - fight that urge. So start by making a simple small version, then extend the solution step by step.
